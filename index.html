Empathy Responses

Practical, trauma-informed language for callers in distress — organized by intensity level.

🟡 Initial Distress — Early Emotional Support
▶

Client is overwhelmed, ashamed, anxious, discouraged, or unsure about treatment — but not expressing immediate danger.

Validation & Rapport
"I'm really glad you reached out today."
"That sounds exhausting, and I can hear how much you've been carrying."
"You're not alone in this."
"A lot of people struggling with addiction feel stuck at first."
"Thank you for being honest with me."
"It takes courage to talk about this."
Reducing Shame
"Addiction does not define who you are."
"Needing help doesn't mean you failed."
"Recovery is rarely a straight line."
"Relapse or setbacks don't erase progress."
"You still deserve support and care."
Encouraging Hope
"Things may feel heavy right now, but change is possible."
"You've already taken an important step by reaching out."
"There are people who genuinely want to help you through this."
"You don't have to figure everything out today."
Reflective Responses
"It sounds like part of you wants things to change, even if another part feels uncertain."
"I hear that you're tired of the cycle."
"It sounds like you've been trying to manage this on your own for a long time."
🟠 Medium Distress — Emotional Crisis
▶

Client expresses hopelessness, severe guilt, relapse, panic, fear, isolation, or emotional breakdown — but no direct suicidal intent yet.

Emotional Containment
"I can hear how overwhelmed you are right now."
"You don't have to go through this moment alone."
"What you're feeling right now sounds incredibly painful."
"I'm here with you."
After Relapse
"Relapsing doesn't mean recovery is impossible."
"A setback can still become part of someone's recovery story."
"This moment doesn't erase the effort you've already made."
"What matters right now is getting support, not punishing yourself."
Hopelessness / Despair
"When people are hurting this much, it can feel like nothing will improve."
"Even if it doesn't feel that way today, this feeling can change."
"You matter, even if addiction has made you feel otherwise."
"You deserve support while you work through this."
Grounding & Stabilization
"Let's focus on getting through this one step at a time."
"Right now, your safety and support matter most."
"You don't need to solve your whole future tonight."
"Can we focus on what would help you feel a little safer or calmer right now?"
Motivational Support
"What's one reason you haven't given up completely?"
"Who in your life has been supportive, even a little?"
"What helped you get through difficult moments before?"
🔴 High-Risk Crisis — Suicidal Statements
▶

Client expresses wanting to die, self-harm thoughts, hopelessness with intent, or statements like "everyone would be better off without me."

Immediate Empathy
"I'm really glad you told me."
"That sounds incredibly painful."
"You don't have to carry this alone right now."
"I'm here with you."
Direct Yet Compassionate Responses
"Your safety is the most important thing right now."
"When someone feels this overwhelmed, getting immediate support can really matter."
"I want to help make sure you stay safe."
"You deserve support through this moment."
If They Say They Want to Die
"I'm sorry you're hurting this deeply."
"I take what you're saying seriously."
"Even though things feel unbearable right now, help is available."
"You are important, and your life has value."
Encouraging Crisis Support
"Would you be open to connecting with a crisis counselor or trusted support person?"
"You don't have to face these thoughts by yourself."
"Reaching out for immediate support can help keep you safe tonight."
⚠️ If Imminent Risk Appears
"Your safety matters more than anything else right now."
"I want to help you stay connected to support."
"Please stay with me while we look at immediate help options."
🛡️ De-escalation Phrases
▶
When Caller Is Angry
"I hear your frustration."
"You've been dealing with a lot."
"I'm not here to judge you."
When Caller Feels Ashamed
"A lot of people feel shame around addiction."
"You're still worthy of help and respect."
When Caller Is Resistant
"It's okay to feel uncertain about treatment."
"You're in control of your decisions."
"We can talk through your concerns together."
When Caller Feels Like a Burden
"You matter."
"People care about what happens to you."
"Reaching out is not a burden."
🚫 Phrases to Avoid
▶

These can accidentally sound dismissive or escalating — never use them.

You just need to stay positive. Others have it worse. Why would you do that? You have so much to live for. Calm down. Everything happens for a reason. You're fine. You need to want recovery more.
🧠 Trauma-Informed Style Tips
▶
✅ Use calm, short sentences
✅ Validate before problem-solving
✅ Avoid arguing with emotions
✅ Don't overpromise outcomes
✅ Ask open-ended questions
✅ Reflect feelings back naturally
✅ Maintain dignity and autonomy
Open-Ended Question Examples
"What's been the hardest part lately?"
"What support feels safest for you right now?"
"What would help you feel less alone tonight?"

Competitor Call Script

Handle callers who ask if you're a competitor facility.

⚠️ Critical Rule
▶
If the caller continues to ask about another facility, the answer is NEVER "Yes" or "No." Repeat Steps 2 & 3 until the caller provides insurance information and completes a clinical assessment.
📋 Step-by-Step Script
▶
STEP 1 — Answer
"Legacy Healing, my name is ___, how can I help?"
STEP 2 — Redirect (REPEAT AS NEEDED)
"We provide treatment to anyone struggling with drugs, alcohol, or mental health. Are you calling for yourself or a loved one?"
STEP 3 — Pivot to Insurance (REPEAT AS NEEDED)
"We have options all throughout the country. We can definitely help! Are you currently covered under private insurance?"
STEP 4 — Admissions Process
"Perfect. Our admissions process has two steps: (1) We review your insurance information to see what options are available and what services are covered. (2) We complete a clinical assessment to determine the best options for your needs. With that in mind, do you have your insurance card handy so we can review your coverage?"
Call Scripts

Methadone / Suboxone / MAT Calls

Understand the caller's intent first — are they looking to Get ON, Stay ON, or Come OFF Methadone/Suboxone?

⚠️ First Step — Identify Caller Intent
▶

Before responding, always determine which of the three situations applies:

GET ON — Caller wants to start Methadone/Suboxone or a MAT program
STAY ON — Caller is already on Methadone/Suboxone and wants to continue
COME OFF — Caller wants to fully detox off Methadone/Suboxone
➕ Caller Wants to GET ON Methadone / Suboxone
▶
Caller
"I'm looking to get on Methadone or a MAT program."
Agent
"I understand and we can definitely help you with that. What exactly are you struggling with?"
Caller
"Yeah, I've been addicted to meth and want to transition into methadone."
Agent
"I'm sorry that you are going through this and it is a good step to seek help. Are you covered by any health insurance?"
🔄 Caller Wants to STAY ON Methadone / Suboxone
▶
Caller
"Are you a methadone clinic or do you offer a MAT program?"
Agent
"I see, and we can help you with that. Are you looking for a methadone clinic to completely come off, or are you looking to get on or stay on methadone?"
Caller
"I want to stay on methadone."
Agent
"We do use methadone at one of our detox facilities, and we also help people get off methadone and Suboxone entirely. Are you covered by any health insurance?"

Still qualify if they have insurance.

✅ Caller Wants to Completely COME OFF Methadone / Suboxone
▶
Caller
"Are you a methadone clinic or do you offer a MAT program?"
Agent
"I see, and we can help you with that. Are you looking for a methadone clinic to completely come off, or are you looking to get on or stay on methadone?"
Caller
"I want to come off of methadone."
Agent
"I'll be happy to assist you, and it is a good step to seek help. Are you covered by any health insurance?"

Still qualify if they have insurance.

Location Questions

Handle callers asking about specific facility locations or states. Do not confirm specific locations — guide them through the assessment process.

⚠️ Key Rule
▶
Do not confirm or deny specific facility locations to callers. Redirect to the specialist assessment process — they will be matched with the best-fit facility after insurance is confirmed.
📋 Step-by-Step Script
▶
STEP 1 — Opening
"Legacy, this is __________. Are you searching for treatment for yourself or a loved one?"
Caller
"Are you located in Montana?"
STEP 2 — Nationwide Coverage
"We do have facilities all throughout the country and we operate from every state. Are you seeking help for yourself or a loved one?"
Caller
"I'm calling for myself, but I want to make sure you have facilities in Montana."
STEP 3 — Specialist Match
"We have facilities across multiple states, and our specialist will match you with the best option for your situation. After they've conducted an assessment, they will provide you the location of the facility well suited for your need. Are you covered by any health insurance?"
Caller
"Yes, I have insurance through my job."
STEP 4 — Transfer
"Perfect. I will transfer you over to the specialist, but first I will gather some basic information."

Meeting Script

Handle callers asking about AA/NA meetings.

📋 Script Flow
▶
1. "Is the meeting for you or a loved one?"
2. "Are you actively struggling or currently sober?"
If actively struggling: "If you're actively struggling, we can help you get the right care. Do you have any insurance?"

Special Call Types

Handling instructions for Drug/DUI classes, Assessments, Sober Living, and more.

🚗 Drug / DUI Classes
▶

Ask: "Do you need this for court or to get your license back? Or are you looking to get help with addiction and go to treatment?"

Court / License: "Unfortunately, we only assist if you are looking to get into treatment as well as provide this information to the court."
Looking for treatment: "Do you have any type of health insurance to help cover the cost of treatment?"
📝 Assessment / Evaluations
▶

Ask: "Do you need one for court, or are you trying to get into treatment for help with addiction?"

Court: "Unfortunately, we only assist if you are looking to get into treatment as well as provide this information to the court."
Looking for treatment: "Do you have any type of health insurance to help cover the cost of treatment?"
🏠 Sober Living / Halfway Houses
▶
🏛️ Halfway Houses
  • • More structured; may be court-mandated
  • • Transitioning from incarceration or inpatient
  • • Set rules, curfews, required programs
🏠 Sober Living Homes
  • • Flexible, peer-supported environments
  • • Not mandated; residents choose to be there
  • • Fewer restrictions than halfway houses
Opening — Understand Their Situation
"I can definitely provide information on sober living, but I'd like to understand your situation a bit more so we can make sure you're set up for success."
Helpful Questions to Ask
"Are you currently using, or have you recently stopped?"
"When was the last time you used?"
"Have you gone through detox before?"
"Are you experiencing any withdrawal symptoms right now?"
"Have you ever been in treatment or rehab?"
"What made you consider sober living at this time?"
"Do you feel stable in your sobriety, or are you looking for more support?"
✅ If Treatment Is Appropriate
"Sober living is a great step, but it's usually most effective after detox or treatment. Based on what you've shared, starting with a program could really help set a strong foundation."
→ Proceed with qualifying script. Ask about insurance to connect them with a specialist.
🏠 If Caller ONLY Wants Sober Living (No Treatment Interest)
"Absolutely, I can help with that. Sober living homes are a great option for maintaining accountability and structure in recovery."
Provide these resources:
Sober Living: SoberLiving.org — nationwide directory
Halfway Houses: FindTreatment.gov — licensed halfway houses
"If anything changes or you decide you'd like to explore treatment options as well, we're here 24/7 to help."
📅 Follow-Up Call (Unsure About Insurance)
▶
"That's completely okay — we can still help. To move forward, we'll just need to confirm their insurance details so we can connect them with the right program. Would you be able to check with them and call us back once you have that information?"
"Perfect! I'll schedule the follow-up call for [day/time]. Once you have the insurance information ready, we'll go over the next steps together and get them the help they need."
Closing: "Thank you so much for reaching out — we really appreciate you taking the time to help them. We'll talk again soon!"
🎖️ Retired Callers
▶

When a caller mentions they are retired, the key step is clarifying their insurance coverage — some retirees have private insurance through their former employer, while others are on Medicare or Medicaid.

Opening Question
"I see that you're retired — can you tell me if you have private insurance through your retirement benefits, or are you covered by Medicare or Medicaid?"
If Unsure
"No problem! Sometimes retirees have private insurance through their former employer, and other times coverage goes directly through Medicare or Medicaid. Can you let me know which one applies to you?"
Outcomes
✅ Private Insurance:
"Great, thank you for clarifying. Let me collect some basic information and send you over to one of our specialists."
❌ Medicare / Medicaid:
"Got it, thank you. Since you're on state-provided coverage, we can offer self-pay options or I can provide information from SAMHSA to help you get the support you need."
💬 Why Insurance / Payment Is Needed
▶
"I completely understand this can feel frustrating, and I want to assure you our goal is to get people the help they need."
"Because treatment involves medical care, housing, meals, and therapy around the clock, we do need insurance or private pay to cover those costs. That way, your loved one can focus entirely on recovery without interruptions."
"If your insurance isn't a fit here, I can help point you toward resources that may accept your plan or provide state-funded options. The most important thing is that your loved one gets help."

Insurance Qualification

Step-by-step script to determine if a caller's insurance qualifies.

📊 Quick Reference Table
▶
Insurance TypeMonthly CostAction
Private PPO$300+✅ Qualified → Transfer to Closer
Marketplace PPO$250–$350Usually Qualified → Transfer to Closer
State Insurance (Medicaid/Medi-Cal)<$100❌ Not Qualified → Provide resources
🔎 Qualification Script
▶
If they pay themselves:
"Okay, great. So is it through the Marketplace, or did you get it directly from a private company?"
If Marketplace:
"Perfect. Do you happen to know if your plan is a PPO, HMO, or EPO?"
If HMO:
"Do you know about how much you pay for your insurance each month?"
If $300 or higher → mark as good private insurance.
If unsure PPO vs HMO:
"No worries — one quick way to check is: do you have to choose from a specific list of doctors, or can you go to any doctor you want?"
Any doctor = PPO. List of doctors = likely HMO or state.
If state insurance:
"Okay, thank you for letting me know. It sounds like that might be state insurance. Unfortunately, we only work with private PPO plans right now, but I can give you some resources that might help."
End call politely or tag as non-qualified.
If caller hesitates on why you're asking:
"The only reason I ask is because the monthly cost usually helps us tell whether it's a state-funded plan or private coverage. State plans usually cost under $100, while private PPOs tend to be $300 or higher. This just helps me make sure I transfer you to the right specialist who can verify your benefits."

💡 Quick Caller Tips
▶
"I got it through healthcare.gov"Marketplace (possible subsidy)
"I have Blue Cross through my job"Private (usually qualified)
"I pay $300+ per month myself"Likely Private PPO → Transfer to Closer
"I pay $50 or $80 a month"State or subsidized → Not qualified

4.3.26 Chat Guidelines — Location, Insurance & Marketplace
▶
📍 Location Inquiries
"We have facilities across multiple states, and our specialist will match you with the best option for your situation. After conducting an assessment, they will provide the location best suited to your needs."
If persistent: "I'm only the chat operator, but if I may ask you a few questions, I can connect you with one of our specialists who can go over the available locations with you."
Do NOT say: "I apologize, but only our specialists are allowed to discuss that." / Do NOT confirm any specific location.
🛡️ Marketplace Insurance
Marketplace plans may be referred out even if fully subsidized — unless the client specifically has Medicaid or Medicare. If unsure, chatter/email: "possible referral please check insurance."
💬 Insurance Question Script
"How do you receive your insurance? Is it through work, a family member, do you pay monthly, or is it through Medicaid or Medicare?"
Always specify Medicaid or Medicare. Do NOT ask if insurance is "free from the state" — some people are state employees. If clarification needed: "Do you work for the state, or is it Medicaid or Medicare?"

3.10.26 Chat Quality Standards & Standard Responses
▶
⏱️ Response Times
Exceeding 60 seconds = late reply. Aim for responses within 30 seconds. Speed shows the patient they are a priority.
💚 Lead with Empathy — Soft Landing
"I'm so glad you reached out."
"I can certainly help you navigate your options today."
"I'm glad you reached out. Are you looking for information for yourself or a loved one?"
"Hi there! I'm [Name]. I'm here to help you navigate your options today. What's on your mind?"
💰 If Asked About Cost / Insurance Coverage
Coverage varies: many plans cover a portion (inpatient, outpatient, PHP) but full coverage is uncommon. There may be co-pays, deductibles, daily limits, or pre-auth requirements. Our insurance specialists can verify exact benefits.
"If you have private insurance, it may cover most of the cost of treatment. If someone does not have insurance, we do offer a self-pay option, which is typically around $10,000/week. Would that be something feasible for you?"
Important: Do NOT immediately mention self-pay. Always confirm private insurance first.
📍 Location Script
"We have multiple locations across the country. May I ask where you're currently located?"
"We service clients in many cities and states. May I ask where you're located?"
After location: "Thank you for sharing. We can take a look at what options may be available in your area. Are you currently covered under any type of insurance?"
Only if caller insists on exact location: "We have multiple locations throughout the country. Location is determined based on where the insurance is accepted. We would need to complete a clinical assessment and verify insurance to determine the appropriate level of care and which locations may be available."

Call Flow Examples
Scenario 1 — Refer Out (Aetna of Illinois)
Caller: I have Aetna.
Agent: "How do you receive that insurance? Is it through work, a family member, do you pay monthly, or is it provided by the state?"
Caller: It's through the state.
Agent: "Is it through Medicare or Medicaid?"
Caller: No, it's Aetna of Illinois.
Agent: "Okay, can I have the member ID and group policy number so we can get it verified and have someone reach out to you? Also, is paying out of pocket an option for you?" (soft pitch)

Caller: Yes, it's through Medicare.
Agent: "As we are a private facility here, we do not work with Medicare. Is paying out of pocket an option for you?"
